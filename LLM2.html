This Week's Macro Walkthrough
12:34 · December 15, 2024

This week's macro disconnect widened: surface resilience in assets, deepening strain in the real economy, and a market structure reliant on a narrow set of supports. The consequential signals came not from index levels, but from households under pressure, small businesses shedding jobs, and a funding complex absorbing heavy issuance.

"While markets focused on index levels, the system registered deteriorating participation—small caps, equal-weight indices, and cyclicals failed to confirm the cap-weighted trend."

Black Friday data confirmed this stress. Nominal sales rose, but item volumes fell while "buy now, pay later" usage surged. Households spent more to acquire less, leaning on embedded leverage.
